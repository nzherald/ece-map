1 Evaluation of The Gardens Early Childhood Education

How well placed is The Gardens Early Childhood Education to promote positive learning outcomes for children?

Not well placed

Requires further development

Well placed

Very well placed

ERO's findings that support this overall judgement are summarised below.

Background

The Gardens Early Childhood Education centre, Hill Road is located near the Auckland Botanic Gardens in Manurewa, Auckland. It offers all day education and care in a renovated villa and is licensed for a maximum of 120 children, including 35 children up to the age of two years. The centre was previously licensed under two separate licences. These have now been merged into one. Children are accommodated in five age-related rooms and outdoor spaces.

The service is part of an organisation that, at present, has one other centre close by in Charles Prevost Drive. These centres are supported by the general manager and the organisation's management team which provides administrative and professional support. The supervisor at the Hill Road centre oversees the day to day management of the centre. The staff, consisting of a core team of qualified and experienced teachers has been recently established. Teachers are strengthening their working relationships. The centre provides a high teacher to child ratio.

The centre caters for a diverse community of mainly local families. The service’s philosophy emphasises a strong partnership between the centre, parents/whānau and children. It reflects the aims of Te Whāriki, the early childhood curriculum.

The centre management have responded positively to recommendations made in the 2011 ERO report.

The Review Findings

The centre is very inclusive in providing education and care for all children. Children are confident, happy and settle quickly to self chosen activities. Where children require extra specialist support, teachers work closely with the relevant external agencies. Parents report that they are very satisfied with the service provided. They take advantage of the many opportunities offered for involvement in the programme and special events.

The programme also offers many opportunities for children to participate in rich literacy, mathematics and science experiences. Children are able to lead their own learning. Teachers support children to extend their interests. The colourful, inviting environments reflect the learning children have experienced while at the centre.

Babies and toddlers are very well cared for in three age-related rooms. Teachers know each child well. Children experience a good flow between the rooms and their designated outdoor areas. Teachers collaborate with parents around children's personal routines and stages of development. Teachers provide opportunities that extend children's explorations and sensory experiences. The indoor environment reflects the language, culture and identity of each child. Children's unique identity is strongly valued by teachers.

Children in the older two year olds to five year olds age-related rooms participate in group projects. This helps to extend some children's interests during formal group times in the morning. Teachers have begun to participate in professional learning and development around extending the interests, dispositions, and skills and knowledge of individual children. Children have opportunities to explore their own interests throughout the day. Children could benefit from teachers reviewing the impact that the formal part of the programme has on outcomes for children.

Teachers have begun to participate in professional learning and development around extending the interests, dispositions, skills and knowledge of individual children. This is becoming more evident in planning and in practices to extend children's interests. This more personalised approach should allow teachers to identify a wider variety of children's learning. They will also be able to use this information as a basis for ongoing planning to extend children's interests.

Portfolios of older children's learning emphasise participation in group activities. Portfolios for the babies and toddlers record the growth and development of each child. There is a growing focus, organisation wide, on identifying individual learning in the stories recorded in portfolios. However this has mainly been around identifying gross and fine motor skill development. It would be beneficial if teachers reviewed the impact this approach is having on outcomes for children.

The centre management values highly the relationships between children, families/whānau and teachers. Teachers reflect the ethnicities of the community and use their home languages in communication with parents and children. Parents and whānau have many opportunities to participate in centre cultural and educational events. Centre management gather parents' perceptions and their aspirations for their children to further inform centre direction.

There are good examples of children's ethnicities being celebrated in the centre. It could now be useful to share this good practice organisation-wide. Te reo Māori is increasingly visible within the environment. Whānau are invited to contribute to the programme. Centre managers are continuing to work to establish relationships with local marae and iwi.

The centre is well managed and well led. The centre management team has a clear vision for the centre. The team's focus is on ensuring high quality performance for the benefit of children and their families. Management systems support ongoing centre improvement. The centre's useful self review processes could be strengthened by evaluating the results of self review against agreed criteria and identified outcomes for children. As part of its regular review cycle, ERO recommends that the teaching team review and define the concepts identified in the centre's philosophy and develop indicators of good practice.

Key Next Steps

ERO and the centre management team agree that key next steps for improvement include:

  • continuing to strengthen programme planning and ongoing assessment to show children’s individual progress in developing skills, dispositions and knowledge
  • continuing to develop teacher confidence with implementing Te Ao Māori
  • implementing the Pasifika Education Plan 2013-2017.

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of The Gardens Early Childhood Education completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist. In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

Next ERO Review

When is ERO likely to review the service again?

The next ERO review of The Gardens Early Childhood Education will be in three years.

The Purpose of ERO Reports

The Education Review Office (ERO) is the government department that, as part of its work, reviews early childhood services throughout Aotearoa New Zealand. ERO’s reports provide information for parents and communities about each service’s strengths and next steps for development. ERO’s bicultural evaluation framework Ngā Pou Here is described in SECTION 3 of this report. Early childhood services are partners in the review process and are expected to make use of the review findings to enhance children's wellbeing and learning.

3 General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

ERO’s Evaluation Framework

ERO’s overarching question for an early childhood education review is ‘How well placed is this service to promote positive learning outcomes for children?’ ERO focuses on the following factors as described in the bicultural framework Ngā Pou Here:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of arotake – self review and of whanaungatanga – partnerships with parents and whānau.

ERO evaluates how well placed a service is to sustain good practice and make ongoing improvements for the benefit of all children at the service.

A focus for the government is that all children, especially priority learners, have an opportunity to benefit from quality early childhood education. ERO will report on how well each service promotes positive outcomes for all children, with a focus on children who are Māori, Pacific, have diverse needs, and are up to the age of two.

For more information about the framework and Ngā Pou Here refer to ERO’s Approach to Review in Early Childhood Services.

ERO’s Overall Judgement and Next Review

The overall judgement that ERO makes and the timing of the next review will depend on how well placed a service is to promote positive learning outcomes for children. The categories are:

  • Very well placed – The next ERO review in four years
  • Well placed – The next ERO review in three years
  • Requires further development – The next ERO review within two years
  • Not well placed - The next ERO review in consultation with the Ministry of Education

ERO has developed criteria for each category. These are available on ERO’s website.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews are tailored to each service’s context and performance, within the overarching review framework. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
