1 Evaluation of the Service

How well placed is the service to promote positive outcomes for children?

Discoveries on Gadsby Rd is developing its capacity to promote positive outcomes for children. 

Context

The service caters for a large number of children from birth to five years of age.  It is situated in a community where Māori, Pacific and very young children predominate.  Centre owners are keen to support these children and their families to engage positively in education.  They plan to continue to work closely with families to establish good foundations for children’s life-long learning. 

This centre is one of three privately owned centres.  Most of the teachers are qualified and registered.  Managers provide ongoing training opportunities to encourage staff to continue to develop professional teaching practice.  This is the centre’s first ERO report.

The Review Findings

Centre managers have established a good range of policies, practices and systems to guide the operation of the centre.  They continuously reflect on ways that they can further improve their management practice.  It would now be useful to refine personnel management systems by:

  • aligning the teacher appraisal system to the registered teacher criteria
  • differentiating job descriptions.

Programme planning includes some good links to Te Whāriki, the New Zealand early childhood curriculum.  Teachers use self-review processes to evaluate the quality of the learning environment and the quality of planning for children’s learning.

They could now consider:

  • shifting the focus of planning from teacher initiated activities, to extending learning through children’s interests
  • building teacher understanding of the cultural identity of Māori and Pacific children so that they can plan more culturally responsive programmes
  • developing a shared understanding of good practice and current early childhood theory to underpin learning programmes. 

Children are settled and engaged in the programme.  Teachers are very attentive to children, and encourage them to make choices from activities provided.  Relationships are respectful and positive.   Good routines, a range of activities and a warm, caring learning environment are provided for children.

Teachers are aware of the need to ensure that the centre environment supports learning effectively. The centre is well maintained, spacious and light, but could be better used to stimulate children’s interests and celebrate learning. 

Teachers should now consider how they can most effectively develop respectful partnerships with parents to support children’s learning and to acknowledge the vital contribution of parents as first teachers.

2 Legal Requirements

Management Assurance on Legal Requirements

Before the review, the staff and management of Discoveries on Gadsby Rd completed an ERO Centre Assurance Statement and Self-Audit Checklist.  In these documents they attested that they have taken all reasonable steps to meet their legal obligations related to:

  • curriculum
  • premises and facilities
  • health and safety practices
  • governance, management and administration.

During the review, ERO looked at the service’s systems for managing the following areas that have a potentially high impact on children's wellbeing:

  • emotional safety (including positive guidance and child protection)
  • physical safety (including supervision; sleep procedures; accidents; medication; hygiene; excursion policies and procedures)
  • suitable staffing (including qualification levels; police vetting; teacher registration; ratios)
  • evacuation procedures and practices for fire and earthquake.

All early childhood services are required to promote children's health and safety and to regularly review their compliance with legal requirements.

ERO is likely to carry out the next review in three years.

General Information about Early Childhood Reviews

About ERO Reviews

The Education Review Office (ERO) is the New Zealand government department that reviews schools and early childhood services throughout New Zealand.

Review focus

ERO's education reviews in early childhood services focus on the factors that contribute to positive learning outcomes for children. ERO evaluates how well placed the service is to make and sustain improvements for the benefit of all children at the service. To reach these findings ERO considers:

  • Pou Whakahaere – how the service determines its vision, philosophy and direction to ensure positive outcomes for children
  • Pou Ārahi – how leadership is enacted to enhance positive outcomes for children
  • Mātauranga – whose knowledge is valued and how the curriculum is designed to achieve positive outcomes for children
  • Tikanga whakaako – how approaches to teaching and learning respond to diversity and support positive outcomes for children.

Within these areas ERO considers the effectiveness of self review and partnerships with parents and whānau.

Review Coverage

ERO reviews do not cover every aspect of service performance and each ERO report may cover different issues. The aim is to provide information on aspects that are central to positive outcomes for children and useful to the service.
